What is Organic Farming?

Use of cover crops, green manures, animal manures and crop rotations to fertilize the soil, maximize biological activity and maintain long-term soil health. Organic farming also uses biological control, crop rotations and other techniques to manage weeds, insects and diseases. It uses rotational grazing and mixed forage pastures for livestock operations and alternative health care for animal wellbeing. It eliminates the use of synthetic pesticides and fertilizers and other materials, such as hormones and antibiotics. It focuses on renewable resources, soil and water conservation, and management practices that restore, maintain and enhance ecological balance.

“What Is Organic Farming?” SARE, 24 Aug. 2020, website.
