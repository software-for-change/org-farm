Why we exist

Promote successful commercial farming in Uganda while increasing access of organic food in every home.

01

THE BEGINNING

I come from a family of farmers. I have seen the benefits of subsistence farming but also seen the challenges that farmers face when they practice commercial farming. Farmers deal with expensive farming processes, limited markets for their food, low quality yield, among many others.

Few years ago, my family started practicing organic farming. We carried out research, started innovating, and experimenting to come up with effective methods of organic farming that would solve some of the challenges we faced. When we put the methods to practise, the results were impressive. Here is an example from some of our crops.

Before Organic Farming After Organic Farming
5 pods per stem per bean plant 20 pods per stem per bean plant
8 pegs per stem per peanut plant 40 pegs per stem per peanut plant

We even saw a reduction of farming costs. We were now spending less on farming procedures yet still seeing an increase in food yields. This set me on a mission, to ensure that affordable organic farming methods are taught and used so that each home in Uganda has access to organic food.

02

The Journey

Successful commercial farming will increase the level of income for farmers in Uganda. We also understand that many reasons affect the expansion of commercial farming. That is why we are dedicated to solving some of these problems so that farmers can make money selling their produce. Some of the solutions we have started working on:

Raining Vegetables will be implemented to provide rural farmers with an online market to sell their food. The reason being most farmers lack access to main local markets and end up losing their quality produce to spoilage. Raining Vegetables will also provide our customers with quality food at affordable prices.

Our consultation services educate farmers about efficient and innovative farming practices thus promoting agricultural research and education. We connect farmers to an expert that teaches them how to spend less on farming processes while yielding sufficient and healthy food. Farmers get a one on one session with an expert which allows them to get advice tailored to their individual farming needs.
